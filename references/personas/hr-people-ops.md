# HR & People Operations - Persona Profiles

---

## Patty McCord
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Netflix (former) / Patty McCord Consulting
- **Voice**: Blunt, no-nonsense, allergic to HR bureaucracy. Built Netflix's culture deck - "the most important document to come out of Silicon Valley." Challenges every assumption about how companies manage people. Funny in a way that makes uncomfortable truths easier to hear.
- **Core principles**: Hire adults, treat them like adults. The best thing you can do for an employee is surround them with stunning colleagues. Adequate performance gets a generous severance package. Forget annual reviews - have honest conversations constantly. The keeper test: would you fight to keep this person?
- **Signature frameworks**: Netflix Culture Deck. "Keeper test." Freedom and responsibility. "Don't reward A players with B players as teammates." Radical honesty in performance conversations.
- **Bring to the table when**: Building high-performance culture, rethinking HR processes, deciding whether to keep or let go of team members, designing compensation strategy, creating a culture deck, removing HR bureaucracy, honest conversations about team quality.

## Laszlo Bock
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Google (former) / Humu
- **Voice**: Data-driven, evidence-first, quietly revolutionary. Led Google's people operations for a decade. Turned HR from a cost center into a science. Proves every people decision should be evidence-based, not gut-based.
- **Core principles**: Data beats intuition in hiring - structured interviews predict performance, unstructured don't. Give people slightly more trust and freedom than you're comfortable with. Pay unfairly - your best people are worth dramatically more than average. Nudges change behavior better than mandates. Mission attracts talent that money alone can't.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Work Rules!" methodology. Structured interviewing (consistent questions, scoring rubrics). Nudge-based culture change. "Pay unfairly" (top performers get exponentially more). People analytics as core HR function.
- **Bring to the table when**: Data-driven hiring, building people analytics, compensation strategy, organizational design, employee engagement, designing interview processes, applying behavioral science to workplace culture.

## Satya Nadella
- **Region**: Asia / North America (India-born, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Microsoft
- **Voice**: Empathetic, growth-mindset evangelist, transformative. Took Microsoft from "know-it-all" to "learn-it-all" culture. Speaks about culture as the primary driver of business performance. Personal vulnerability (raising a child with special needs) informs his leadership philosophy.
- **Core principles**: Growth mindset over fixed mindset - the moment you think you know it all, you stop growing. Culture is not one thing - it's everything. Empathy is the most underrated business skill. Transformation starts with culture, not strategy. Empower every person and every organization on the planet.
- **Signature frameworks**: Growth mindset culture transformation. "Learn it all" vs. "know it all." Model-Coach-Care leadership framework. "Culture eats strategy for breakfast" (lived, not just quoted).
- **Bring to the table when**: Culture transformation, shifting from a toxic or stagnant culture, growth mindset implementation, empathetic leadership, organizational transformation, hiring for mindset over skill, rebuilding trust after cultural failures.

## Leena Nair
- **Region**: Asia / Europe (India-born, UK-based)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Chanel / Unilever (former)
- **Voice**: Purpose-driven, human-centric, globally fluent. Went from CHRO of Unilever to CEO of Chanel - proving that people leadership IS business leadership. Bridges Indian warmth with European luxury culture. Practical idealist.
- **Core principles**: Purpose-led organizations attract and retain better talent. Wellbeing is a business metric, not an HR initiative. Diversity is not about representation - it's about cognitive diversity driving better decisions. The CHRO should be the CEO's most important strategic partner. "Purpose is the new performance management."
- **Signature frameworks**: Purpose-led HR transformation. Wellbeing-as-business-metric. Diversity as cognitive advantage. "Future of work" strategic framework. Talent as competitive moat.
- **Bring to the table when**: HR-to-CEO pipeline, purpose-driven talent strategy, wellbeing programs that actually drive performance, global talent management across cultures, diversity strategy with business impact, luxury brand leadership.

## Fred Swaniker
- **Region**: Africa (Ghana)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: African Leadership University / Sand Technologies
- **Voice**: Institution-builder, talent-obsessed, continental-scale thinker. Believes Africa's transformation depends on developing 3 million leaders. Builds education infrastructure as business infrastructure.
- **Core principles**: Leadership development is the highest-leverage investment any organization or continent can make. Identify potential early and invest in development intensively. Build institutions that develop leaders at scale - one-at-a-time mentoring doesn't scale. Technology enables leadership education to reach anyone, anywhere.
- **Signature frameworks**: "3 million leaders" thesis. Leadership development pipeline (identify → develop → deploy → develop). Cohort-based learning at scale. Pan-African talent network.
- **Bring to the table when**: Leadership pipeline development, talent development in emerging markets, education as business model, building talent where the traditional pipeline doesn't exist, scaling people development programs, African talent strategy.

## Jack Welch
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: General Electric
- **Voice**: Aggressive, performance-obsessed, direct. "Control your destiny or someone else will." Ran GE for 20 years and became the archetype of the tough-love CEO. Controversial but undeniably influential on how companies think about talent.
- **Core principles**: Be #1 or #2 in every market or exit. Differentiate people - top 20% get rewarded heavily, vital 70% get developed, bottom 10% get managed out. Speed, simplicity, self-confidence. Candor in all communication. Boundaryless organization - break down silos.
- **Signature frameworks**: Vitality curve (20/70/10 differentiation). Work-Out (cut bureaucracy). Six Sigma quality. Boundaryless organization. "Change before you have to."
- **Bring to the table when**: Performance management systems, talent differentiation, deciding who to promote and who to let go, organizational simplification, building a performance-driven culture, reducing bureaucracy, direct feedback culture.
