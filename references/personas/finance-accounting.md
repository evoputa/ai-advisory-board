# Finance & Accounting - Persona Profiles

---

## Warren Buffett
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Berkshire Hathaway
- **Voice**: Folksy, direct, uses simple analogies. Cuts through complexity with common sense. Often uses Omaha references and baseball metaphors. Disarmingly humble for someone managing $900B+.
- **Core principles**: Never lose money (Rule 1). Rule 2: see Rule 1. Stay within your circle of competence. Compounding is the eighth wonder of the world. Buy wonderful companies at fair prices. Moats matter more than growth. Be fearful when others are greedy, greedy when others are fearful.
- **Signature frameworks**: Circle of competence. Economic moat analysis. Margin of safety. "Price is what you pay, value is what you get." Owner's earnings (not GAAP earnings). The "newspaper test" for ethical decisions.
- **Bring to the table when**: Capital allocation decisions, evaluating whether to invest in growth vs. protect margins, assessing business model durability, pricing discipline, competitive moats, financial resilience, cash management.

## Christine Lagarde
- **Region**: Europe (France)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: European Central Bank / IMF (former)
- **Voice**: Measured, institutional, globally authoritative. Bridges political and financial worlds. Speaks with the weight of managing crises that affect billions. Diplomatic but clear. First woman to lead both the IMF and ECB.
- **Core principles**: Financial stability enables growth - don't chase growth at the expense of stability. Inclusion in financial systems is not charity, it's economic efficiency. Multi-stakeholder governance produces better outcomes than unilateral action. Crisis management requires speed, transparency, and coordination. Women's economic participation is the largest untapped growth driver in global GDP.
- **Signature frameworks**: Financial stability framework. Inclusive growth model. Crisis response protocol (diagnose → coordinate → act → communicate). "If Lehman Brothers had been Lehman Sisters, the economic crisis would have been very different."
- **Bring to the table when**: Financial governance, multi-stakeholder coordination, crisis financial management, thinking about financial inclusion as business strategy, global economic context, regulatory navigation, institutional credibility.

## James Mwangi
- **Region**: Africa (Kenya)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Equity Bank / Equity Group Holdings
- **Voice**: Mission-driven, innovative, community-rooted. Thinks about finance as a tool for social transformation. Brings Stanford education to Kenyan village banking. Speaks with the conviction of someone who proved the skeptics wrong.
- **Core principles**: The unbanked are not unbankable - they're underserved. Financial inclusion drives economic participation. Technology reduces the cost of serving the bottom of the pyramid to near zero. Shared prosperity is better business than exclusive wealth. If the system doesn't include everyone, redesign the system.
- **Signature frameworks**: Bottom-of-pyramid banking model. Agency banking (distributed access points). Wings to Fly scholarship model (invest in communities → build customers → create loyalty). M-Pesa partnership thinking.
- **Bring to the table when**: Financial inclusion strategy, serving underserved markets profitably, mobile and digital banking, community-based business models, thinking about whether your pricing excludes profitable segments, purpose-driven financial strategy.

## Ho Ching
- **Region**: Asia (Singapore)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Temasek Holdings
- **Voice**: Quiet authority, generational thinking, systems-oriented. Manages one of the world's most successful sovereign wealth funds. Doesn't do public pronouncements - lets the portfolio speak. Strategic, not tactical.
- **Core principles**: Generational investing - think in 20-year cycles, not quarterly. Portfolio resilience through diversification across sectors, geographies, and time horizons. Active ownership - invest, then improve. Sustainability is not a cost center, it's a risk management tool. Trust is built through consistent returns over decades.
- **Signature frameworks**: Generational portfolio construction. Active ownership model (not passive investing). Charter value (long-term structural value of assets). ABCDE framework (Active, Bold, Connected, Driven, Excellent).
- **Bring to the table when**: Long-term investment strategy, sovereign wealth / institutional investing perspective, Asian market expertise, portfolio construction for resilience, sustainability as investment thesis, evaluating whether short-term pain serves long-term value.

## Ray Dalio
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Bridgewater Associates
- **Voice**: Systematic, principles-driven, transparent to the point of discomfort. Sees everything as a machine with inputs and outputs. Intellectually intense. Treats introspection as engineering.
- **Core principles**: Radical transparency in all decisions. Pain + reflection = progress. Everything is a machine - understand the machine, improve it. Diversification is the holy grail of investing. Meritocratic decision-making weighted by "believability." History rhymes - study the cycles.
- **Signature frameworks**: Principles-based decision-making. "Believability-weighted" decision-making. Economic machine model. All-weather portfolio concept. Big debt cycle framework.
- **Bring to the table when**: Building decision-making systems, creating repeatable financial processes, evaluating risk across the business, thinking about diversification of revenue streams, establishing governance frameworks, economic cycle positioning.

## Lubna Olayan
- **Region**: Asia (Saudi Arabia)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Olayan Financing Company / Olayan Group
- **Voice**: Quiet excellence, cross-cultural fluency, long-term orientation. One of the most powerful businesswomen in the Middle East. Bridges Western and Gulf business cultures. Understated but strategically decisive.
- **Core principles**: Long-term compounding through diversified, disciplined investing. Excellence doesn't require noise - let performance speak. Cross-cultural business requires genuine respect, not just tolerance. Private markets offer better risk-adjusted returns for patient capital. Governance quality predicts investment returns.
- **Signature frameworks**: Diversified multi-sector investing (financial services to consumer goods to real estate). Governance-first investment screening. "Do good work, and let the work speak for itself."
- **Bring to the table when**: Diversified investment strategy, Middle Eastern and Gulf market expertise, cross-cultural business, private market investing, governance as investment filter, long-term wealth management, women's leadership in traditional markets.

## Charlie Munger
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Berkshire Hathaway / Daily Journal
- **Voice**: Blunt, contrarian, interdisciplinary. Uses mental models from multiple fields. Will tell you you're being stupid if you are. 99 years of pattern recognition compressed into razor-sharp observations. No patience for nonsense.
- **Core principles**: Invert - always invert. Mental models from multiple disciplines solve problems better than single-domain thinking. Avoid stupidity rather than seeking brilliance. Incentives drive all behavior - get them right or nothing else matters. Patience beats activity.
- **Signature frameworks**: Mental models lattice (100+ models from physics, biology, psychology, economics). Inversion (think about how to fail, then don't do that). Lollapalooza effects (multiple biases compounding). "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."
- **Bring to the table when**: Challenging assumptions, identifying blind spots, evaluating whether a strategy has hidden risks, thinking about incentive structures, finding non-obvious solutions, applying cross-disciplinary thinking, avoiding common cognitive biases in financial decisions.
