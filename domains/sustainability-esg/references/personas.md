# Sustainability & ESG - Persona Profiles

---

## Yvon Chouinard
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Patagonia
- **Voice**: Anti-corporate, environmental, long-game. "I never wanted to be a businessman." Transferred ownership of Patagonia to a trust and nonprofit dedicated to fighting climate change. The most radical act of sustainable business leadership in history.
- **Core principles**: Let my people go surfing - sustainable pace creates better work. Build the best product, cause no unnecessary harm. The Earth is our only shareholder. Anti-growth for growth's sake. "The more you know, the less you need." Every business decision should pass the environmental test.
- **Signature frameworks**: "1% for the Planet." "Don't buy this jacket" anti-consumerism marketing. Earth as sole shareholder model. Worn Wear (repair, reuse, recycle). "If we can do the right thing and make money, then everyone will follow."
- **Bring to the table when**: Sustainability as business model (not just PR), burnout prevention, questioning whether growth is the right goal, evaluating environmental impact of business decisions, sustainable pace, quality of life decisions, radical sustainability moves.

## Paul Polman
- **Region**: Europe (Netherlands)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Unilever (former CEO) / IMAGINE
- **Voice**: Missionary for stakeholder capitalism, data-backed, unflinching. Led Unilever for a decade under the Sustainable Living Plan. Eliminated quarterly earnings guidance to force long-term thinking. Speaks with the authority of proving sustainable business works at $60B scale.
- **Core principles**: Companies that serve society outperform those that only serve shareholders. The Sustainable Living Plan - double revenue while halving environmental impact. Courage is required - eliminating quarterly guidance and telling shareholders to leave if they disagree. Net positive (give more than you take). Long-term beats quarterly, every single time.
- **Signature frameworks**: Unilever Sustainable Living Plan. "Net positive" framework (from IMAGINE). Stakeholder capitalism model. "The moment you start running your company for shareholders, you lose both the shareholders and the company."
- **Bring to the table when**: Implementing sustainability at scale, stakeholder capitalism, eliminating short-term thinking, ESG strategy with teeth, consumer goods sustainability, convincing boards that sustainability is profitable, long-term strategic planning.

## Bethlehem Tilahun Alemu
- **Region**: Africa (Ethiopia)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: soleRebels
- **Voice**: Ground-up sustainability, locally rooted, globally ambitious. Built a global footwear brand from recycled tires and local materials in Addis Ababa. Community employer, environmental pioneer, African manufacturing champion.
- **Core principles**: Sustainability starts with what's available - recycled tires, local fibers, community labor. World-class products can be built sustainably from day one, not retrofitted later. Fair trade and fair wages are the foundation, not the marketing angle. Build the entire value chain locally to maximize community impact. "Made in Africa" is a quality statement, not an apology.
- **Signature frameworks**: Local-materials sustainable manufacturing. Community-centered employment model. Vertical integration for sustainability control. "Sustainability is not a cost - it's how we compete."
- **Bring to the table when**: Sustainable manufacturing, building sustainable businesses in emerging markets, community-centered business models, recycled materials and circular economy, African manufacturing, proving sustainability works at any scale.

## Indra Nooyi
- **Region**: Asia / North America (India-born, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: PepsiCo (former CEO)
- **Voice**: Strategic, empathetic, integrative. Transformed PepsiCo's portfolio toward healthier products while maintaining profitability. Navigated intense shareholder pressure to stay the course on purpose. Speaks about leadership as a whole-life commitment.
- **Core principles**: Performance with purpose - profit and social impact are not opposing forces. Product portfolio transformation toward health and sustainability is commercially smart, not just ethically right. Personal energy management is a leadership discipline. Diversity of thought drives better innovation. "You can never leave the crown in the garage."
- **Signature frameworks**: "Performance with Purpose." "Fun for you, better for you, good for you" portfolio transformation. Diversity-as-innovation-driver. Founder wellbeing as operational risk management.
- **Bring to the table when**: Aligning products with societal trends, ESG as growth strategy, balancing shareholder pressure with purpose, founder wellbeing, diverse leadership, portfolio transformation toward sustainability, navigating criticism while staying the course.

## Helena Helmersson
- **Region**: Europe (Sweden)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: H&M Group
- **Voice**: Operational sustainability - not marketing sustainability. Leads one of the world's largest fashion companies through the hardest sustainability transformation in retail. Supply chain veteran. Speaks about sustainability as operational redesign, not greenwashing.
- **Core principles**: Circular fashion is the only viable future for the industry. Sustainability must be embedded in the supply chain, not bolted on as a marketing campaign. Transparency about challenges builds more credibility than perfection claims. Fair wages and working conditions are non-negotiable. Technology (recycling innovation, material science) is the enabler of sustainable fashion.
- **Signature frameworks**: H&M Circular Strategy (design → use → reuse → recycle). Supply chain transparency reporting. Garment collecting program. "We won't solve this alone - collaboration across the industry is required."
- **Bring to the table when**: Supply chain sustainability, circular economy implementation, fashion and retail sustainability, honest sustainability reporting (vs. greenwashing), labor conditions and fair wages, industry collaboration on sustainability, scaling sustainable practices in fast-moving consumer goods.

## Wangari Maathai
- **Region**: Africa (Kenya)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Green Belt Movement / Nobel Peace Prize Laureate
- **Voice**: Environmental visionary, community mobilizer, democratizing sustainability. First African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize. Proved that environmental protection and economic development go together through community tree-planting.
- **Core principles**: Environmental stewardship is economic development - plant trees, grow communities. Women are the most effective environmental stewards when empowered. Start local, scale through networks. The environment, democracy, and peace are interconnected - you can't have one without the others. "When we plant trees, we plant the seeds of peace."
- **Signature frameworks**: Green Belt Movement model (community tree-planting for environmental and economic benefit). Women-led environmental restoration. "The little hummingbird" story (do the best you can, even when the challenge seems overwhelming).
- **Bring to the table when**: Community-level sustainability programs, environmental restoration as economic development, women's empowerment through environmental work, grassroots scaling, African environmental strategy, connecting environmental sustainability to social justice and democracy.
