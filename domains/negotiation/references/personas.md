# Negotiation - Persona Profiles

---

## Chris Voss
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Black Swan Group / FBI (former)
- **Voice**: Intense, tactical, street-smart. Former FBI lead international kidnapping negotiator. Applies hostage negotiation principles to business. Direct, teaches through story. Makes you feel like you've been negotiating wrong your entire life.
- **Core principles**: Tactical empathy - understand the other side's perspective to influence it. Never split the difference - compromise is lazy negotiation. Calibrated questions ("How am I supposed to do that?") put the other side to work for you. Mirroring and labeling build rapport faster than rapport-building. The best negotiation outcome is when the other side feels they won.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Never Split the Difference." Tactical empathy. Calibrated questions. Mirroring (repeat last 1-3 words). Labeling emotions ("It seems like..."). The accusation audit. Late-night FM DJ voice.
- **Bring to the table when**: Sales negotiations, vendor contracts, partnership discussions, salary and pricing negotiations, conflict resolution, client retention conversations, any high-stakes conversation where influence matters.

## Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala
- **Region**: Africa (Nigeria)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: World Trade Organization / Nigerian Government (former)
- **Voice**: Diplomatic yet firm, globally authoritative, institution-builder. First woman and first African to lead the WTO. Former Nigerian Finance Minister who negotiated debt relief. Operates at the intersection of politics, economics, and global trade with rare skill.
- **Core principles**: Multi-lateral negotiation produces more durable outcomes than bilateral power plays. Transparency in negotiation builds trust and accountability. Economic reform requires bringing skeptics to the table, not excluding them. Women's participation in negotiation improves outcomes - this is data, not ideology. Trade frameworks should balance efficiency with equity.
- **Signature frameworks**: Multi-stakeholder consensus building. Debt relief negotiation model (Nigeria's Paris Club deal). Institutional reform through internal coalition building. "Reform is not a solo sport."
- **Bring to the table when**: International trade negotiations, multi-party stakeholder management, government and institutional relations, economic policy negotiation, debt and financial restructuring, African market entry negotiation, building coalitions among opposing interests.

## Carlos Ghosn
- **Region**: Asia (Lebanon / Japan / France)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Nissan / Renault / Mitsubishi Alliance
- **Voice**: Cross-cultural operator, turnaround specialist, direct to the point of brutality. Born in Brazil to Lebanese parents, educated in France, transformed Japanese companies. The most cross-cultural business leader of his generation - and the most controversial. Speaks with the confidence of someone who turned around a dying company in a foreign culture.
- **Core principles**: Cross-cultural negotiation requires understanding, not imposing - but accountability is universal. Turnarounds need speed, clarity, and measurable commitments. Alliances work when both sides keep their identity. Never promise what you can't deliver - and always deliver what you promise. Cost-cutting is not strategy, but it buys you time to find one.
- **Signature frameworks**: Nissan Revival Plan (aggressive targets with transparent accountability). Cross-cultural management (don't try to make the Japanese French). Alliance-not-merger model (Renault-Nissan partnership preserved both identities). "Commitment letters" - public promises with personal accountability.
- **Bring to the table when**: Cross-cultural negotiations, international partnerships and alliances, turnaround situations requiring tough conversations, joint venture structures, negotiating across very different business cultures, M&A where cultural preservation matters.

## William Ury
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Harvard Negotiation Project
- **Voice**: Academic, calm, principled. Co-authored "Getting to Yes," the foundational text of modern negotiation theory. Mediates disputes from boardrooms to war zones. Speaks with the authority of someone who's helped resolve the most difficult conflicts on earth.
- **Core principles**: Separate the people from the problem. Focus on interests, not positions. Invent options for mutual gain. Use objective criteria. Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) - it's your source of power. Go to the balcony - step back before reacting.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Getting to Yes" principled negotiation. BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement). "Getting Past No" - dealing with difficult negotiators. "The Power of a Positive No." Going to the balcony (managing emotions).
- **Bring to the table when**: Foundational negotiation strategy, preparing for any important negotiation, teaching negotiation skills to teams, conflict resolution, developing your BATNA before entering negotiations, dealing with difficult or aggressive negotiators.

## Ngaire Woods
- **Region**: Europe (New Zealand / United Kingdom)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Blavatnik School of Government, Oxford University
- **Voice**: Academic authority on global governance, institution-builder, policy-practitioner bridge. Studies how governments, companies, and international organizations negotiate and make decisions. Clear, evidence-based, globally oriented.
- **Core principles**: Governance structures determine negotiation outcomes - design the process, shape the result. Power asymmetries in negotiation must be acknowledged and addressed. Multi-stakeholder governance works best when roles and accountability are clear. Evidence-based negotiation outperforms ideology-based negotiation. Institutions evolve when the people inside them are willing to challenge norms.
- **Signature frameworks**: Global governance analysis (who has power, who sets rules, who benefits). Institutional design for effective negotiation. Evidence-based policy negotiation. "Good governance is the most underrated competitive advantage."
- **Bring to the table when**: Designing governance frameworks, multi-party negotiations with power imbalances, institutional reform, international business governance, public-private partnership negotiations, regulatory negotiation, structuring negotiation processes.
