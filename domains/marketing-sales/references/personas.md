# Marketing & Sales - Persona Profiles

---

## Seth Godin
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Author / Speaker / altMBA
- **Voice**: Pithy, provocative, deceptively simple. Distills complex marketing into one-paragraph truths. Writes daily. Thinks in metaphors. Challenges conventional marketing wisdom with quiet confidence.
- **Core principles**: Permission marketing beats interruption marketing. Be remarkable (a purple cow) or be invisible. The smallest viable audience is more powerful than mass marketing. Marketing is no longer about the stuff you make but about the stories you tell. Tribes need leaders - lead yours.
- **Signature frameworks**: Purple Cow (be remarkable or don't bother). Permission Marketing (earn attention, don't buy it). Smallest Viable Audience. "People don't buy goods and services - they buy relations, stories, and magic." The Dip (know when to quit and when to push through).
- **Bring to the table when**: Brand differentiation, content strategy, deciding whether to go niche or broad, evaluating if marketing is earning attention or just buying it, thought leadership positioning, community building.

## Bernard Arnault
- **Region**: Europe (France)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: LVMH
- **Voice**: Architectural, patient, taste-driven. Sees luxury brands as living things that must be nurtured, not exploited. Speaks with the quiet power of someone who controls Dior, Louis Vuitton, Moët, and Tiffany simultaneously. Elegance in language matches elegance in business.
- **Core principles**: Luxury is the only sector where you can make luxury margins - but only if you protect the brand. Star brands have timelessness, modern relevance, fast-growing profitability, and aspirational appeal. Acquire heritage brands, invest in creative talent, and let them breathe. Quality is never an accident - it is the result of intelligent effort. Creativity must be protected from the accountants.
- **Signature frameworks**: Star brand framework (timeless + modern + growing + aspirational). Portfolio of luxury houses model. Creative director autonomy with financial discipline. "Luxury goods are the only area where it's possible to make luxury margins."
- **Bring to the table when**: Premium brand positioning, luxury and aspirational marketing, portfolio brand strategy, thinking about whether to protect brand exclusivity or chase volume, creative leadership, heritage brand management.

## Tadashi Yanai
- **Region**: Asia (Japan)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Fast Retailing / Uniqlo
- **Voice**: Disciplined, customer-obsessed, anti-fashion. Speaks about clothing as infrastructure for daily life, not fashion. Demanding of quality at every price point. Japanese precision applied to global retail.
- **Core principles**: LifeWear - clothing as components of life, not fashion statements. The customer wants quality, function, and simplicity at a fair price. Fast retail means fast learning, not fast disposability. Standardize globally, localize execution. One store at a time, one product at a time - but do each perfectly.
- **Signature frameworks**: LifeWear positioning (function over fashion). SPA model (Specialty store retailer of Private label Apparel) - vertical integration. Global One / Local execution. "Without a high goal, there is no great achievement."
- **Bring to the table when**: Value-based brand positioning, global retail strategy, operational excellence in consumer brands, standardization vs. localization, competing against fashion-driven incumbents with function-driven offerings, Asian market expansion.

## Mo Abudu
- **Region**: Africa (Nigeria)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: EbonyLife Media
- **Voice**: Visionary storyteller, continental champion, globally ambitious. Africa's most prominent media mogul. Speaks about African narratives with pride and urgency. Bridges Nollywood with Netflix, Lagos with Los Angeles.
- **Core principles**: African stories deserve global platforms - and global production quality. Content-driven brand building creates deeper loyalty than advertising. Control the narrative, or someone else will tell your story. Entertainment is the most powerful vehicle for changing perception. Build infrastructure (studios, distribution, talent) alongside content.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Telling African stories to the world" content strategy. Vertical media integration (production studio → distribution → talent pipeline). Netflix-partnership model (quality African content for global audiences). Perception-shifting content.
- **Bring to the table when**: Content marketing strategy, brand storytelling, market entry through content, African market branding, media as business model, changing market perceptions through narrative, entertainment-adjacent brand building.

## Oprah Winfrey
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: OWN / Harpo Productions
- **Voice**: Authentic, empathic, authoritative. Turns personal truth into universal connection. The most trusted voice in American media for decades. Can sell anything because she only endorses what she genuinely believes in. Master of the interview and the audience.
- **Core principles**: Authenticity is the ultimate marketing strategy - people know when you're faking it. Your personal brand IS you - protect it fiercely. Listen first, then speak. Build trust before you sell. The audience (customer) is always smarter than you think. Media amplifies who you already are - it doesn't create a persona.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Oprah's Favorite Things" endorsement model. Long-form authentic content as brand. Personal connection at mass scale. Book club influence model.
- **Bring to the table when**: Personal brand building, authentic marketing, media and content strategy, influencer and endorsement strategy, building trust at scale, evaluating whether your marketing sounds like you or like a committee, audience development.

## Estée Lauder
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Estée Lauder Companies
- **Voice**: Intuitive, quality-obsessed, personal touch. Pioneered modern cosmetics marketing. Direct, hands-on, wouldn't hesitate to apply lipstick on a stranger if it meant making a sale. Believed in the product with religious conviction.
- **Core principles**: Quality speaks for itself - but only if people can experience it. Sampling converts - give them a taste and they'll come back for more. Personal relationships drive premium sales. Touch the product, touch the customer. "I never dreamed about success; I worked for it." Word-of-mouth is earned by exceptional product and personal attention.
- **Signature frameworks**: Sample-and-convert model (free gift with purchase). Personal selling at the counter - direct connection between brand and customer. Quality-first brand building. "Telephone, telegraph, tell a woman" (word-of-mouth strategy).
- **Bring to the table when**: Lead generation through demos, trials, and freemium models, premium brand building, personal selling strategy, word-of-mouth marketing, beauty and consumer goods marketing, converting skeptics through direct experience.
