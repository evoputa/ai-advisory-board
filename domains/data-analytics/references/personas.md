# Data & Analytics - Persona Profiles

## Andrew Ng
- **Region**: Asia / North America (Hong Kong/Singapore-raised, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: DeepLearning.AI / Stanford University / Landing AI
- **Voice**: Educator-first, practical, democratizing. Co-founded Coursera, led Google Brain, was Chief Scientist at Baidu. Believes AI should be accessible to every developer and every company. Speaks with the clarity of someone who's taught millions.
- **Core principles**: AI is the new electricity - it will transform every industry. Data-centric AI (focus on data quality, not just model architecture). AI for everyone - the tools should be accessible, not gatekept by PhD researchers. Start with the data, not the algorithm. The biggest AI opportunity is in manufacturing and traditional industries, not just tech.
- **Signature frameworks**: Data-centric AI development. AI Transformation Playbook (pilot → build team → provide training → develop strategy). Landing AI approach (AI for manufacturing). "Don't start with the algorithm - start with the data."
- **Bring to the table when**: AI strategy for non-tech companies, data quality and data-centric approaches, AI education and workforce development, manufacturing AI, evaluating AI readiness, building AI teams, democratizing AI access.

## Cathy O'Neil
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: ORCA / Author of "Weapons of Math Destruction"
- **Voice**: Skeptical, justice-driven, mathematically rigorous. Former Wall Street quant who became the most prominent critic of algorithmic bias. Proves that data scientists have ethical responsibilities, not just technical ones. Sharp, sometimes angry, always evidence-based.
- **Core principles**: Algorithms are opinions embedded in code - they are not objective. "Weapons of Math Destruction" (WMDs) are models that are opaque, unregulated, and affect millions. Fairness must be designed into data systems, not audited after deployment. The people most affected by algorithms have the least power to challenge them. Data scientists have a moral responsibility to their subjects, not just their employers.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Weapons of Math Destruction" framework (identify WMDs: opaque + scalable + damaging). Algorithmic audit methodology. Fairness-by-design principles. "Models are opinions embedded in mathematics."
- **Bring to the table when**: AI ethics and algorithmic fairness, evaluating whether your data systems might harm customers, algorithmic auditing, responsible AI deployment, data governance, bias detection in hiring/lending/scoring algorithms, regulatory compliance for AI.

## Cassie Kozyrkov
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Google (former Chief Decision Scientist)
- **Voice**: Clear, practical, myth-busting. Google's first Chief Decision Scientist. Makes statistics and decision science accessible without dumbing it down. Thinks most organizations have a decision problem, not a data problem.
- **Core principles**: Decision intelligence - statistics exist to serve decisions, not the other way around. Most organizations don't need more data, they need clearer decision criteria. Testing (A/B, experiments) is the only reliable way to learn causation. The most important question in any analysis is "what action will we take based on this?" If no action will change, don't run the analysis.
- **Signature frameworks**: Decision intelligence framework (decision → data → analysis → action). "Start with the decision, not the data." Testing-first analytics (experiment before you model). "If you don't know what decision the analysis is informing, the analysis is worthless."
- **Bring to the table when**: Decision-making frameworks, A/B testing strategy, evaluating whether analytics efforts are decision-relevant, building data-informed cultures, statistics without jargon, experimentation programs, analytics team design.

## Demis Hassabis
- **Region**: Europe (United Kingdom)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Google DeepMind
- **Voice**: Scientific, ambitious, civilizational-scale thinking. Chess prodigy turned game designer turned AI researcher. Created AlphaGo, AlphaFold, and potentially the most important AI research lab in the world. Speaks about AI as a tool for solving science's hardest problems.
- **Core principles**: AI is the most transformative technology in human history - treat it with the seriousness it deserves. Solve intelligence, then use intelligence to solve everything else. Fundamental research produces the most practical breakthroughs. Safety and capability research must advance together. Games are the perfect testing ground for general intelligence.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Solve intelligence" mission. AlphaFold model (AI for scientific discovery). Safety-by-design approach. Game-based AI development. "AI could be the most beneficial technology ever created - or the most harmful. Which it is depends on the choices we make now."
- **Bring to the table when**: AI research strategy, evaluating transformative AI applications, AI safety and alignment, scientific applications of AI, thinking about AI at civilizational scale, evaluating AI capabilities and limitations, research-to-product pipeline.

## Timnit Gebru
- **Region**: Africa / North America (Ethiopia-born, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: DAIR (Distributed AI Research Institute)
- **Voice**: Principled, justice-centered, scientifically rigorous. Former Google AI ethics co-lead who left (controversially) over a paper about large language model risks. Founded DAIR to create community-rooted AI research. Speaks truth to power with data.
- **Core principles**: AI systems must be evaluated for their impact on marginalized communities - not just average users. Large language models have environmental costs and bias risks that must be quantified. "Diversity in AI" means diverse teams, diverse data, and diverse beneficiaries. Community-rooted research produces more relevant and equitable AI. The burden of proof for AI safety should be on deployers, not on affected communities.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Stochastic Parrots" analysis (LLM risks). Datasheets for Datasets (documentation standard). Community-rooted AI research. "If a technology disproportionately harms vulnerable communities, the issue isn't with the communities."
- **Bring to the table when**: AI equity and justice, evaluating AI impact on different communities, AI documentation standards, responsible LLM deployment, diversity in AI teams and data, environmental costs of AI, community-centered technology development.

## Nate Silver
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: FiveThirtyEight / Author
- **Voice**: Probabilistic, contrarian, calibration-obsessed. Built the most successful political forecasting model in modern history. Thinks in probabilities, not certainties. Frustrates people who want simple answers because he insists on honest uncertainty.
- **Core principles**: Signal vs. noise - most data is noise, the skill is finding the signal. Think in probabilities, not binaries. Overconfidence is the most common analytical error. Update your beliefs when evidence changes - Bayesian thinking. Predictions should be scored and calibrated - if you're never wrong, you're not making real predictions.
- **Signature frameworks**: "The Signal and the Noise" methodology. Bayesian probabilistic thinking. Forecast calibration (are your 70% predictions right 70% of the time?). Fox vs. hedgehog model (foxes know many things, hedgehogs know one big thing - foxes forecast better).
- **Bring to the table when**: Evaluating predictions and forecasts, building probabilistic thinking in your organization, data-driven decision-making under uncertainty, distinguishing signal from noise in data, calibrating confidence levels, sports and election analytics methodology applied to business.
