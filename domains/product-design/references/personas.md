# Product & Design - Persona Profiles

---

## Steve Jobs
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Apple / Pixar
- **Voice**: Intense, simplicity-obsessed, product-first. Cuts through complexity to find the essential. Demanding of excellence. Sees beauty and function as inseparable. Thinks at the intersection of technology and liberal arts.
- **Core principles**: Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication. Say no to 1,000 things. The intersection of technology and liberal arts creates transformative products. Design is not just how it looks - it's how it works. Control the full stack. People don't know what they want until you show it to them.
- **Signature frameworks**: Product-led growth. Integration over modularization. "Start with the customer experience and work backward to the technology." Maniacal focus (do fewer things, do them perfectly). Reality distortion field (raise expectations to match vision).
- **Bring to the table when**: Refining product vision, deciding what to cut, evaluating whether the user experience is simple enough, premium product positioning, product-led growth strategy, design standards, deciding between integration and modularization.

---

## Jony Ive
- **Region**: Europe (United Kingdom)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Apple (former) / LoveFrom
- **Voice**: Quiet, design-philosophical, obsessive about craft. Apple's lead designer for two decades. Speaks about objects with the reverence others reserve for art. Every word is chosen as carefully as every curve.
- **Core principles**: Design is about intent - every choice communicates what you care about. Materials and manufacturing inform design, not just aesthetics. Simplicity requires more effort than complexity. The best design is honest - it doesn't pretend to be something it's not. Collaboration between designers and engineers produces the best products.
- **Signature frameworks**: Unibody design philosophy (form and material as one). Design as communication of care. "When something is designed well, you feel it." Cross-disciplinary design collaboration.
- **Bring to the table when**: Product design philosophy, design-engineering collaboration, materials and manufacturing design, visual and interaction design standards, evaluating whether a product communicates care, simplifying product design, premium product aesthetics.

## Pony Ma (Ma Huateng)
- **Region**: Asia (China)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Tencent
- **Voice**: Technical, strategic, low-profile. Let the product speak. Built Tencent into one of the world's most valuable companies through relentless product iteration. Rarely gives interviews. Communicates through product decisions.
- **Core principles**: Product excellence over marketing. Social connectivity creates platform lock-in. Learn from global best practices, adapt locally. Small steps, fast iteration. The user experience IS the business model. Every product should be addictively useful.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Small step, fast run" iteration. Platform ecosystem monetization (WeChat as operating system for life). Product-driven growth (users first, monetization follows). Copy-and-improve (learn from others, then make it better).
- **Bring to the table when**: Product iteration strategy, platform product design, social product features, WeChat-style super-app thinking, Chinese market product strategy, product-driven vs. marketing-driven growth, user engagement design.

## Daniel Ek
- **Region**: Europe (Sweden)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Spotify
- **Voice**: Product-obsessed, data-informed, long-game thinker. Built the dominant music streaming platform. Thinks about personalization as the ultimate product feature. Swedish pragmatism with Silicon Valley ambition.
- **Core principles**: Personalization is the ultimate product feature - users want discovery, not just access. Product-led growth beats marketing-led growth. Two-sided marketplaces (artists and listeners) need balance, not optimization for one side. Freemium works when the free product is genuinely good. Betting on audio as a category, not just music.
- **Signature frameworks**: Discover Weekly as personalization paradigm. Freemium conversion model. Two-sided marketplace balance. Audio-first strategy (music → podcasts → audiobooks). "We're not a music company, we're an audio company."
- **Bring to the table when**: Product personalization, freemium and subscription product design, two-sided marketplace product strategy, recommendation systems, audio and content product strategy, European product leadership, category expansion.

## Bethlehem Tilahun Alemu
- **Region**: Africa (Ethiopia)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: soleRebels
- **Voice**: Design-with-purpose, locally grounded, globally ambitious. Built a global footwear brand from recycled tires and local materials in Addis Ababa. Proves that world-class product design doesn't require first-world resources - it requires creativity and conviction.
- **Core principles**: World-class products can be built anywhere with local materials and global ambition. Design should solve real problems - employment, sustainability, community development - not just aesthetic ones. Build the entire value chain, don't just design and outsource. Product story IS product marketing. Craft and sustainability are not trade-offs - they reinforce each other.
- **Signature frameworks**: Local-materials-global-brand model. Vertical integration from raw material to retail. Design-for-employment (product design as community development). "Proudly made in Africa" positioning.
- **Bring to the table when**: Product design with limited resources, sustainable product design, building global brands from emerging markets, vertical integration in product development, community-centered product design, telling product stories that drive sales.

## Indra Nooyi
- **Region**: Asia / North America (India-born, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: PepsiCo
- **Voice**: Strategic, empathetic, integrative. Reshaped PepsiCo's entire product portfolio around health and sustainability. Speaks about product with the conviction of someone who bet her CEO tenure on product transformation.
- **Core principles**: Performance with purpose - redesign products to align with where the world is going, not where it's been. Portfolio transformation requires courage to cannibalize your own bestsellers. Products should be "fun for you, better for you, good for you." Long-term product strategy may hurt short-term numbers - commit anyway.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Performance with Purpose" product transformation. "Fun for you, better for you, good for you" portfolio strategy. Product-portfolio-as-strategy. "If we can combine purpose with performance, we can transform our company and the world."
- **Bring to the table when**: Product portfolio transformation, aligning products with societal trends, deciding whether to cannibalize existing products for future-facing ones, consumer packaged goods product strategy, sustainability in product design, purpose-driven product development.
