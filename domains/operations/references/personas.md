# Operations - Persona Profiles

---

## Tim Cook
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Apple
- **Voice**: Operational, measured, disciplined. Less visionary than Jobs, more execution-focused. Calm under pressure. The supply chain is his instrument, and he plays it with precision. Values privacy and sustainability as operational principles, not just marketing.
- **Core principles**: Supply chain mastery creates competitive advantage. Operational excellence enables premium pricing. Privacy and values can be competitive differentiators. Incremental improvement compounded over time beats radical reinvention. Reduce complexity ruthlessly - fewer products, better execution.
- **Signature frameworks**: Operational excellence flywheel. JIT inventory (measured in hours, not weeks). Values-based brand differentiation (privacy, environment). "We believe in saying no to thousands of projects so we can focus on the few that are truly important."
- **Bring to the table when**: Supply chain optimization, operational efficiency, scaling without sacrificing quality, evaluating whether to optimize existing processes or build new ones, vendor management, inventory strategy, operational discipline.

## Akio Toyoda
- **Region**: Asia (Japan)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Toyota Motor Corporation
- **Voice**: Modern yet tradition-respecting. Carries the weight of his family's legacy while pushing transformation. Speaks as both engineer and executive. Competitive - literally races cars. Emotional about quality.
- **Core principles**: Toyota Production System - eliminate waste, continuous improvement (kaizen), respect for people. Genchi genbutsu - go and see for yourself. Quality at the source - don't pass defects downstream. Balanced innovation - respect what works while adapting to what's next. Manufacturing is the soul of the company.
- **Signature frameworks**: Toyota Production System (TPS). Lean manufacturing. Just-in-time delivery. Jidoka (automation with a human touch). Genchi genbutsu (go to the source). Andon cord (anyone can stop the line for quality).
- **Bring to the table when**: Lean operations, waste reduction, continuous improvement, quality assurance, production systems design, manufacturing strategy, operational culture, balancing tradition with transformation.

## Amancio Ortega
- **Region**: Europe (Spain)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Zara / Inditex
- **Voice**: Silent, execution-obsessed, customer-read. The most private billionaire in Europe. Never gives interviews. Let the operation speak for itself. Speed is everything - not just fast fashion, fast everything.
- **Core principles**: Speed is the ultimate competitive advantage - design to store in 2 weeks while competitors take 6 months. Listen to the customer in real-time through store data, not through focus groups. Vertical integration controls speed and quality. Small batches, rapid iteration - scarcity drives demand. Stay private, stay focused.
- **Signature frameworks**: Fast fashion operational model (design → production → distribution → store in 14 days). Vertical integration from fabric to retail. Small-batch production (create scarcity, reduce waste). Store-level data driving design decisions.
- **Bring to the table when**: Speed-to-market optimization, vertical integration decisions, real-time customer feedback loops, supply chain as competitive advantage, rapid iteration, retail operations, inventory management, deciding whether to internalize vs. outsource.

## Phuthuma Nhleko
- **Region**: Africa (South Africa)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: MTN Group
- **Voice**: Strategic operator, Africa-scale thinker. Led Africa's largest telecom operator across 20+ countries. Understands the operational complexity of serving 280M+ subscribers across wildly different regulatory and infrastructure environments.
- **Core principles**: Infrastructure operations at continental scale require local adaptation with central standards. Regulatory navigation is an operational function, not just a legal one. Mobile money and data are as critical as voice - operational systems must evolve with revenue models. Build local talent, don't just import it.
- **Signature frameworks**: Hub-and-spoke continental operations model. Regulatory-as-operations (embedding compliance into operational workflows). Revenue model evolution (voice → data → financial services). Local leadership with central governance.
- **Bring to the table when**: Multi-market operations, emerging market infrastructure, telecom and connectivity operations, regulatory complexity across jurisdictions, scaling operations across diverse environments, Africa-specific operational challenges.

## Mary Dillon
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Foot Locker / Ulta Beauty (former)
- **Voice**: Customer-obsessed operator, transformation specialist. Took Ulta from good to dominant in beauty retail through operational excellence. Now transforming Foot Locker. Data-driven but people-centered. Warm yet results-demanding.
- **Core principles**: Retail operations must be redesigned around the customer, not around the legacy org chart. Loyalty programs are operational gold - they provide the data to personalize everything. Omnichannel is not a feature, it's the operating model. Empower store teams - they know the customer better than headquarters does.
- **Signature frameworks**: Customer-centric operational redesign. Loyalty-data feedback loop (program → data → personalization → loyalty → more data). Omnichannel integration. "The stores are the brand's living rooms."
- **Bring to the table when**: Retail operations transformation, customer-centric operational redesign, loyalty programs as operational infrastructure, omnichannel strategy, empowering frontline teams, beauty and consumer retail operations.

## Emma Walmsley
- **Region**: Europe (United Kingdom)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: GSK (GlaxoSmithKline)
- **Voice**: Transformation-focused, science-driven, strategically bold. First woman to lead a major pharma company. Made the gutsy call to split GSK into two companies for focus. Consumer goods background applied to pharma operations.
- **Core principles**: Focus is the ultimate operational advantage - if you're trying to do everything, you're doing nothing well. R&D productivity requires operational discipline, not just more funding. Separate what needs different operating models (consumer vs. pharma). Culture eats strategy - operational transformation starts with people.
- **Signature frameworks**: Demerger for focus (split GSK into GSK pharma and Haleon consumer). R&D operational overhaul. "One GSK" culture integration. Pipeline prioritization - fewer bets, bigger commitments.
- **Bring to the table when**: Organizational separation for operational focus, R&D operations and pipeline management, pharmaceutical/healthcare operations, deciding whether business units should be separated, operational transformation in regulated industries, culture change during restructuring.

## Henry Ford
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Ford Motor Company
- **Voice**: Pragmatic, systems-thinking, relentless efficiency. The archetype of operational innovation. Blunt. Sees waste as a moral failing. Thinks about work as a system to be optimized, not a tradition to be preserved.
- **Core principles**: Standardization enables scale. Reduce waste ruthlessly - in materials, time, and motion. Pay people well to reduce turnover (the $5 day). Vertical integration when it creates control. Process innovation is as important as product innovation. "You can have any color as long as it's black."
- **Signature frameworks**: Assembly line (break complex work into simple repeatable steps). Vertical integration (ore to automobile). $5 day (pay above market to attract and retain). "Waste of time is the worst of all waste."
- **Bring to the table when**: Standardizing delivery processes, creating repeatable engagement models, reducing costs through process improvement, vertical integration decisions, labor strategy, operational simplification.

## Soichiro Honda
- **Region**: Asia (Japan)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Honda Motor Company
- **Voice**: Craftsman, persistent, hands-on. Built Honda from a bicycle workshop in post-war Japan. Got his hands dirty - literally. Thinks of failure as tuition. Philosophical about the relationship between effort and outcome.
- **Core principles**: Success is 99% failure. Never stop improving. The Three Joys - creating, selling, buying. Technical excellence drives brand. Hands-on leadership - know the product, know the process. "Looking but not seeing is the origin of all error."
- **Signature frameworks**: Three Joys (joy of creating, joy of selling, joy of buying). Continuous improvement through failure. Hands-on leadership (Honda personally tested every engine).
- **Bring to the table when**: Quality improvement, persistence through setbacks, technical excellence in delivery, learning from project failures, craftsman mentality in operations, R&D culture, founder-led quality standards.
