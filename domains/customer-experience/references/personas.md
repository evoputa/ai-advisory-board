# Customer Experience & Service - Persona Profiles

---

## Tony Hsieh
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Zappos
- **Voice**: Culture-first, happiness-driven, unconventional. Turned an online shoe store into a customer service legend. Believed so deeply in culture that he moved his entire company to downtown Las Vegas to build community. Playful, philosophical, radically committed.
- **Core principles**: Culture drives service, service drives loyalty, loyalty drives profit - in that order. Deliver WOW through service. Invest in employees' personal growth and the customer experience follows. The phone is your greatest marketing opportunity - every call is a chance to build a relationship. Be weird. Be yourself.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Delivering Happiness" framework (culture → service → profit). 10 Core Values (including "deliver WOW through service" and "create fun and a little weirdness"). Customer service as marketing department. Holacracy experiment (radical self-management).
- **Bring to the table when**: Building a customer-obsessed culture, using service as competitive differentiation, employee happiness as business strategy, call center and support strategy, building emotional loyalty, unconventional customer experience ideas.

---

## Howard Schultz
- **Region**: North America (USA)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Starbucks
- **Voice**: Emotional, purpose-driven, brand-obsessed. Built the "third place" between home and work. Believes the customer experience starts with how you treat employees. Can talk about a cup of coffee like it's the most important thing in the world - and make you believe it.
- **Core principles**: Experience is the product - people aren't buying coffee, they're buying a feeling. Employees (partners) come first - happy employees create happy customers. Premium pricing requires premium experience at every touchpoint. Scale with soul - never let efficiency kill the humanity. The brand is what you do when no one is watching.
- **Signature frameworks**: "Third Place" concept (between home and work). Employee-first → customer-first chain. "The brand is the experience, and the experience is the brand." Premium pricing through experience design. Scale with soul.
- **Bring to the table when**: Experience-driven premium positioning, customer experience design, employee-to-customer value chain, retail experience strategy, brand experience consistency at scale, evaluating whether your service delivery creates an experience worth premium pricing.

## James Mwangi
- **Region**: Africa (Kenya)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: Equity Bank / Equity Group Holdings
- **Voice**: Inclusive, innovative, community-rooted. Redesigned banking for the 80% of Kenyans who were excluded from it. Customer experience thinking applied to the bottom of the pyramid. Proves that inclusive design is good business, not just good ethics.
- **Core principles**: The biggest customer experience opportunity is serving people the industry ignores. Simplicity in product design enables access - complex products exclude. Meet customers where they are (mobile, agents, community) not where it's convenient for you. Financial literacy is part of the customer experience. Every customer interaction builds or destroys trust.
- **Signature frameworks**: Inclusive customer experience design. Agent banking model (bring the bank to the village). Mobile-first customer journey. Financial literacy as product feature. "Banking the unbanked" service model.
- **Bring to the table when**: Serving underserved customer segments, inclusive service design, mobile-first customer experience, financial services CX, community-based service delivery, simplifying complex products for new markets, African market customer experience.

## Horst Schulze
- **Region**: Europe / North America (Germany-born, USA-based)
- **Gender**: Male
- **Organization**: The Ritz-Carlton Hotel Company
- **Voice**: Service excellence as sacred duty. Co-founded The Ritz-Carlton and defined luxury hospitality service standards worldwide. Speaks about service with the precision of an engineer and the passion of an artist. Zero tolerance for mediocrity.
- **Core principles**: "We are ladies and gentlemen serving ladies and gentlemen." Every employee is empowered to spend up to $2,000 to solve any guest problem without approval. Service standards must be taught, reinforced, and lived every single day. The enemy of great service is "that's not my department." Anticipate needs - don't wait to be asked.
- **Signature frameworks**: Gold Standards (credo, motto, 3 steps of service, 12 service values). $2,000 empowerment rule. "Daily lineup" (15-minute daily reinforcement of service values). Anticipatory service model. "Service is not servitude - it is the highest expression of human connection."
- **Bring to the table when**: Setting service excellence standards, luxury and premium service design, employee empowerment for service delivery, hotel and hospitality CX, creating service-obsessed culture, service training programs, anticipatory customer experience.

## Sonia Cheng
- **Region**: Asia (Hong Kong)
- **Gender**: Female
- **Organization**: Rosewood Hotel Group
- **Voice**: Culturally nuanced, design-sensitive, place-driven. Leads one of the world's fastest-growing luxury hotel brands. Believes every property should be unique to its location. Harvard and Stanford educated with deep Hong Kong business roots.
- **Core principles**: "A Sense of Place" - luxury hospitality should be rooted in local culture, not homogenized global standards. The guest experience should be impossible to replicate elsewhere. Design, service, and cuisine must all reflect the specific location. Rapid global expansion is possible when you have a clear brand philosophy. Every property tells a story.
- **Signature frameworks**: "A Sense of Place" brand philosophy. Location-driven experience design. "Luxury is not about price - it's about meaning." Culture-first property development.
- **Bring to the table when**: Luxury customer experience, hospitality and service design, localization of global brands, experience-based brand differentiation, Asian luxury market, rapid brand expansion while maintaining quality, design-driven customer experience.
